Welcome, Future Student-Athletes and International Scholars!


Studying and participating in sports in the U.S. can be an amazing experience, but it comes with its own challenges. Whether you're here to play sports or earn a degree, this guide will provide you with the key tips to help you succeed academically, socially, and athletically in the U.S.


Adapting to the U.S. Culture

When you first arrive in the U.S., it can feel overwhelming. From understanding cultural norms to getting used to the academic system, here are some tips to make your transition smoother:

  • Join international student groups to meet people with similar backgrounds.
  • Attend campus events to get involved in university life.
  • Learn about U.S. communication styles, such as directness and small talk.
  • Keep an open mind and be patient with yourself as you adjust to new cultural norms.


Finding Scholarships for International Students & Athletes

Scholarships can make studying and competing in the U.S. more affordable. Here’s where to look:

  • Check with the university’s financial aid office for international student-specific scholarships.
  • Explore athletic scholarships by talking to your coach or sports department.
  • Search online scholarship databases such as Fastweb, Scholarship.com, and Niche.
  • Apply for external scholarships offered by foundations, governments, and international organizations.


Succeeding as a Student-Athlete

Balancing academics and athletics can be demanding, but it’s not impossible. Here are some strategies to help you thrive:

  • Stay organized: Use a planner or calendar to track both academic deadlines and sports schedules.
  • Communicate with your professors: Let them know your athletic commitments so they can support you when conflicts arise.
  • Maintain a healthy lifestyle: Nutrition, sleep, and physical fitness are key to staying on top of both school and sports.
  • Stay positive and resilient: Sports can teach you valuable lessons in discipline and perseverance that will help you succeed in the classroom as well.

Prioritizing Mental Health

Adapting to a new country, especially as a student-athlete, can be stressful. It’s important to take care of your mental health by:

  • Utilizing counseling services available on campus.
  • Building a support network of friends and teammates.
  • Practicing mindfulness techniques such as meditation and yoga to relieve stress.
  • Being open to talking about mental health, which is an important part of maintaining overall well-being.
