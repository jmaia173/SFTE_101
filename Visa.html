What is a Student Visa?

A student visa allows international students to legally study in the United States. For those who wish to study and play sports, such as soccer, the F-1 visa is the most common choice. It allows students to study, work part-time on campus, and participate in college sports teams.


Types of U.S. Student Visas
  • F-1 Visa: For academic students attending universities, colleges, high schools, and other accredited educational institutions.
  • M-1 Visa: For vocational students attending non-academic institutions such as technical schools.
  • J-1 Visa: For exchange visitors, including those on educational or cultural exchange programs, like sports exchange programs.

F-1 Visa Process

The F-1 visa is the most popular choice for international students. Here's a quick rundown of the application process:

  1. Apply to a U.S. institution: Get accepted into an SEVP-certified school.
  2. Get your I-20 Form: The school will provide you with an I-20 form, required for your visa application.
  3. Pay the SEVIS Fee: Pay the SEVIS fee online.
  4. Complete the DS-160: Fill out the DS-160 form online.
  5. Schedule Your Visa Interview: Set up an interview at your local U.S. embassy or consulate.
  6. Attend the Interview: Bring your DS-160 confirmation, I-20 form, passport, fee receipt, and proof of financial support.
  7. Visa Approval: If approved, your visa will be stamped into your passport.

Student Visa for Athletes

Athletes on an F-1 visa have unique opportunities and responsibilities while studying and playing sports in the U.S. Here's an overview of key points:

  • Sports Scholarships: Many U.S. universities offer sports scholarships based on athletic performance. These can help cover tuition and living costs.
  • Eligibility to Play: To compete, athletes must meet requirements set by organizations like the NCAA, NAIA, or NJCAA, including academic standards and amateur status.
  • Work-Study Opportunities: F-1 students can work on-campus, including in sports-related roles such as athletic support or coaching assistant. Off-campus work may also be allowed with specific authorization like CPT or OPT.
  • Recruitment Process: Athletes can be recruited by U.S. colleges through showcases, tournaments, or recruitment agencies. It's important to connect with coaches and programs early on.
  • Maintaining Visa Status: Athletes must balance academic performance and athletic commitments to maintain F-1 status and eligibility to play.
